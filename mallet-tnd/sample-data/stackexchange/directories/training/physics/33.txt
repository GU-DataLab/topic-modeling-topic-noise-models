The average speed is always (total length) &divide; (total time). In your case, the total time can be calculated as \begin{align} \text{time}_1 &amp;= \frac{120 \mathrm{miles}}{40 \mathrm{mph}} \\\\ \text{time}_2 &amp;= \frac{120 \mathrm{miles}}{60 \mathrm{mph}} \end{align} so the total time is $120\mathrm{miles} \times \left(\frac1{40\mathrm{mph}} + \frac1{60\mathrm{mph}}\right)$. The average speed is therefore: \begin{align} \text{average speed} &amp;= \frac{2 \times 120\mathrm{miles}}{120\mathrm{miles} \times \left(\frac1{40\mathrm{mph}} + \frac1{60\mathrm{mph}}\right)} \\\\ &amp;= \frac{2 }{ \left(\frac1{40\mathrm{mph}} + \frac1{60\mathrm{mph}}\right)} \\\\ &amp;= 48 \mathrm{mph} \end{align} In general, when the length of the trips are the same, the average speed will be the harmonic mean of the respective speeds. $$ \text{average speed} = \frac2{\frac1{v_1} + \frac1{v_2}} $$
