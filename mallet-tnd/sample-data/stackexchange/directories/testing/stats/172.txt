 Sample size doesn't much depend on the population size, which is counter-intuitive to many. Most polling companies use 400 or 1000 people in their samples. There is a reason for this: A sample size of 400 will give you a confidence interval of +/-5% 19 times out of 20 (95%) A sample size of 1000 will give you a confidence interval of +/-3% 19 times out of 20 (95%) When you are measuring a proportion near 50% anyways.
